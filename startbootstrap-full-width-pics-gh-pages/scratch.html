Oxford University

My long time childhood obsession

Reasons Why I Love Oxford

  1. The History
    • It was founded in 1096

    • The oldest university in the English-speaking world

    • The University of Oxford is made up of 43 constituent colleges and was school to many lords of England


  2. The Alumni
    • Many of my favorite scholars and authors attended or taught at Oxford at some point

    • JRR Tolkien and CS Lewis used to have philosophical debates right off campus at a pub called to Eagle and Crown

    • This is thought to be where they first came up with the competition to see who could write the best christian novel, resulting in Tolkien's Lord of the Rings and CS Lewis's Chronicles of Narnia


  3. The Architecture
    • It follows the architectural style of the Romanesque and Gothic. On the exterior are layers of blind arcading with rounded and pointed arches, typical of Romanesque and Gothic architecture respectively, as well as crenellated walls and buttressing.

    • Essentially, if Howarts from Harry Potter was a town it would be Oxford

    • In fact, there are many sites on campus where Harry Potter was filmed including the Divinity School, New College, Christ Church College and the Bodleian Library which was founded in 1602 by Sir Thomas Bodley, it is one of the oldest libraries in Europe. With over 13 million printed items.


  4. The Traditions
    • They still teach in the tutor system as they taught the lords of history. Which means no lectures. Your tutor teaches you your entire degree one on one.

    • In order to go and take a test you have to wear your full Oxford robes. Since in the past no other language was spoken at Oxford besides latin, it is not rare to walk past a latin street sign and students in long robes chatting back and forth in latin.

    • They still hold regular white tie balls at Oxford where girls must come in full gowns and guys in tuxedos with tails.
